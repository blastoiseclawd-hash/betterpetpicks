## Key Patterns from Research

### 1. The 2-3 inch consensus (not 3-4 inch as existing page claims)
Vet-reviewed sources, expert blogs, and the most upvoted Reddit comments all converge on **2-3 inches** as the standard recommendation. The existing content's "3-4 inches for clumping" is on the high end and contradicts most authoritative sources. Need to revise.

Litter-Robot specifically warns: too much litter causes cycling issues and weight sensor problems. Their fill line is around 3 inches.

### 2. The "pickup test" heuristic
Multiple owners independently mention: **you should be able to pick up the litter box**. If you can't lift it, you've put in too much. This is an excellent practical test that the existing content doesn't include.

### 3. Cats fling/scatter litter when there's too much
Across all 4 threads, owners report that **excess litter triggers more flinging behavior**. "Otherwise they go berserk and fling litter everywhere." Cats dig more aggressively in deeper litter, which sends litter over the sides.

### 4. The "halfway in" behavior is diagnostic
Pam Johnson-Bennett's well-known observation: when litter is too deep, **cats put only their front half in the box** because the surface feels unstable. This is one of the most reliable signs and almost no consumer content mentions it.

### 5. Excessive consumption can signal a health issue
The 66-lb-per-month thread had multiple commenters urging a vet visit — **excessive urination is a sign of diabetes** in cats. This is a genuinely important addition to a "how much litter" page that nobody else writes about.

### 6. The "top off vs dump" controversy
Two camps in the community:
- **Top off only** (modern, with good clumping litter): scoop daily, top off weekly, full change every 1-3 months. Some report not doing a full change for over a year.
- **Regular dumps** (traditional): full change weekly or biweekly regardless of clumping
The honest answer is: with high-quality clumping litter and consistent scooping, the dump interval can stretch significantly. The existing page is too dogmatic about "every 2-4 weeks."

### 7. "Boulder clumps" stuck to the box bottom
A specific failure mode: when the cat pees repeatedly in the same spot, clumps merge into giant masses that stick to the bottom. Solved by **scooping more often** (twice a day), not by adding more litter. The existing content treats stuck clumps as a depth issue, but it's also a frequency issue.

### 8. Daily scooping is non-negotiable for behavior
Across multiple threads, owners report: **miss a day of scooping and the cat finds somewhere else to go**. Squishy fabric (couch, laundry pile, sleeping bag, closet) is the #1 alternative target. This is an emotional pain point worth meeting head-on for first-time owners.

### 9. Wet food affects litter consumption
Cats on wet food urinate more (they're getting more moisture). A diet change can dramatically alter monthly litter usage. Not in the existing content.

### 10. Some cats dig to the bottom regardless
Owner reports: "One of mine doesn't dig much and the other digs until the bottom no matter how much litter is there." Individual cat behavior varies wildly. The existing page is too prescriptive.

## Content Assessment

### What the existing page does well
- Covers depth by litter type (clumping, non-clumping, crystal, pellet, corn-based)
- Has good "what happens when too much/too little" framing
- Includes monthly consumption estimates by household size
- Has a topping-off process

### What needs to change
1. **Depth recommendations are too high.** Existing says "3-4 inches for clumping" — should be "2-3 inches" per vet-reviewed sources. Adjust all content.
2. **Missing the "pickup test."** Add this practical heuristic.
3. **Missing the "halfway in" diagnostic.** Add this as a sign of too-deep litter.
4. **Missing the health flag.** When monthly consumption suddenly spikes for one cat, that's a vet visit, not a litter problem.
5. **Voice is generic-expert, not the new REASSURANCE posture.** The Cat Care Essentials desk now writes like a friend who remembers being a confused new owner. Existing content is too clinical.
6. **Too dogmatic about full-change frequency.** The honest range is wider than "every 2-4 weeks."
7. **Missing the "miss a day = pee on the couch" warning.** First-time owners need this framed empathetically — not as scolding, but as "here's what happens, here's how to make it not happen."
8. **Wet food / urination volume connection.** Worth mentioning.

### Voice rework priorities (REASSURANCE posture)
- Lead with permission to keep it simple ("you're overthinking this")
- Acknowledge first-time uncertainty as normal
- Use the "you're fine" energy — not expert authority
- Replace "the answer depends on the litter type" cold opener with empathy
- Keep specifics (depths, amounts) but frame them as "here's what works" not "here's the rule"

### Structure recommendation
Keep the 6-section structure but reorder/rewrite:
1. **Quick answer (calm)** — 2-3 inches for most litter, "you can stop researching"
2. **The pickup test** (NEW) — practical heuristic, you can stop guessing
3. **What happens when too much** — include "halfway in" behavior
4. **What happens when too little** — keep, soften framing
5. **Topping off without overfilling** — keep with steps
6. **Monthly usage + when to worry** (NEW health flag) — "if it suddenly doubles, that's a vet question"
7. **Depth by litter type** — keep but adjust numbers
