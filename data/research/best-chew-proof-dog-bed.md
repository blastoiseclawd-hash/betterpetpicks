## Key Pattern Findings

### The Chewing-Out-of-Boredom vs Destructive Intent Distinction
The single most important framing: these beds work for dogs that chew when bored or anxious. They do NOT work for dogs with true destructive intent (usually separation anxiety, untreated). For dogs with severe separation anxiety, the correct answer is a professional behaviorist + possibly medication + crate during alone-time — not a $150 bed to destroy.

### The Shelter Standard
Kuranda beds are what shelters use. When a rescue with an anxious dog is fostering or boarding thousands of animals, they need beds that survive. Shelters overwhelmingly use Kuranda. This is real-world durability testing at scale. Recommendation stands.

### Aluminum Frame > Steel Frame
Aluminum doesn't rust, is lighter to move, and has comparable chew-resistance (dogs can't chew either). Steel frames work indoors but corrode outdoors. For any bed used on patios, porches, or in outdoor kennels, aluminum wins.

### The Padded-Cot Breakthrough
For years, chew-proof beds meant "no padding." Either a rigid mesh cot (Kuranda) or a padded floor bed that doesn't really survive chewing (PetFusion). The FXW TitanNest is part of a new category: elevated cots with integrated padded sleeping surfaces and hidden-zipper covers. This is the first time you can have durability AND comfort in one bed.

### Zippers Are the Failure Point
On any padded bed that claims "chew-proof," the zipper is the weak link. Dogs target zippers because they're the only visually distinct element. YKK zippers hold up better than generic zippers. Hidden zippers (flaps that cover the pull) are better still. Any listing that doesn't mention zipper construction is hiding that it uses cheap zippers.

### Replacement-Fabric Philosophy
Kuranda sells replacement Cordura panels separately, so you replace the surface without replacing the frame. This is a long-term ownership advantage that competitors don't offer. The frame becomes a 10-year purchase rather than a 2-year one.

### Chew-Proof Guarantees
K9 Ballistics advertises a chew-proof guarantee with replacement. Worth understanding what it covers: typically the first chewing incident within 90 days of purchase. Not lifetime. Not unlimited. Still a real benefit for the first-time buyer.

### What Doesn't Work (Competitor Shortcuts)
- **"Indestructible" memory foam beds** — foam compresses then gets chewed through stitching. There is no truly indestructible foam bed.
- **Plastic basket beds** — dogs chew through the plastic rim. Not recommended.
- **Crate pads marketed as chew-proof** — virtually none actually are. These are for dogs that don't chew.

## Angles Competitors Miss

1. **Chewing out of boredom vs destructive intent.** Most articles don't distinguish.
2. **Zipper quality.** Never mentioned, but it's the real failure point.
3. **Replacement-fabric option.** Kuranda's long-term advantage.
4. **The new padded-cot category.** Most articles still treat elevated = no padding.
5. **When to use a crate instead.** For truly destructive dogs, the crate is safer than any bed.
