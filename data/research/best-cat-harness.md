## Key Pattern Findings

### Escape Mechanics (The Actual Failure Mode)
Cats escape harnesses by one of three moves:
1. **Backing out.** They back up while the leash is taut. If the neck strap is not tight enough, the harness slides over the head.
2. **Houdini twist.** They flatten their body and rotate, letting the harness slip sideways. Wrap-style harnesses with body coverage defeat this.
3. **Chewing through the leash.** Less common but happens with bungee-style leashes under stress.

**Fit rule:** "Two-finger" test for neck and chest straps — you should just barely fit two fingers between strap and fur. Tighter than most owners intuitively set it.

### Vest vs H-strap vs Wrap
- **H-strap** (e.g., PetSafe Come With Me Kitty): simplest to put on, easiest to escape. Works for calm cats in enclosed yards, not for outdoor Houdini types.
- **Step-in vest** (Voyager): cat steps into it, then buckles close on the back. Moderate escape resistance. Fails when cat backs up hard and slips forward.
- **Wrap vest** (Kitty Holster, PUPTECK): full body coverage, hook-and-loop or buckle closure. Best escape resistance. Harder to put on a reluctant cat.
- **Adjustable vest** (rabbitgoo): vest with multiple adjustment straps. Middle ground on both escape resistance and ease of application.

### The Acclimation Curve (Universally Skipped)
Most owners buy a harness, put it on, and expect the cat to walk. Reality: cats need 2-4 weeks of indoor acclimation before the first outdoor attempt.
- Day 1-3: harness on, no leash, 5 minutes indoors with high-value treats.
- Day 4-7: harness on longer, cat moves around freely indoors.
- Day 8-14: attach leash, let it drag indoors. No tension.
- Day 15+: first outdoor attempts, short distances from door.

Skip this and the cat flops sideways, refuses to walk, or panics. Owners then conclude "my cat hates harnesses" when the actual issue is skipped training.

### The Flop Problem
Many cats go limp the first time they wear a harness. This is not a harness failure — it is a novel-sensation freeze. Continue the acclimation schedule; most cats work through it.

### Sizing Runs Small to Large Inconsistently
Every brand sizes differently. A "medium" rabbitgoo is not a "medium" PetSafe. Always measure your cat's chest (the widest point behind the front legs) before ordering. Sizing charts in listings are reliable; brand name categories ("small", "medium") are not.

## Angles Competitors Miss

1. **Two-finger fit test.** Most articles say "adjust for comfort" without a standard. Two fingers is the documented rule from shelter and vet sources.
2. **Acclimation schedule.** Competitors list products but skip the 2-4 week training curve. Almost every first-time harness failure traces to skipped acclimation.
3. **The flop is normal.** Treating "my cat went limp" as a product failure sells more harnesses but does not help the reader.
4. **Sizing inconsistency across brands.** Charts over labels. Measure the cat.
5. **When NOT to use a harness.** Panicky cats, outdoor-aggressive cats, and cats with respiratory conditions should not wear harnesses. Say so.
