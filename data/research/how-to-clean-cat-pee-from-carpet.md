## Key Patterns from Top-Voted Comments

### Why Cat Pee Is Uniquely Hard
- Cat urine contains **uric acid crystals** that regular cleaners don't break. White vinegar is the acid that breaks them.
- Concentrated urine — little water relative to particulate — is why it smells orders of magnitude worse than other pee.
- If you use a plain soap/detergent cleaner, you can appear to clean the stain visually but leave the uric acid crystals intact. Humidity reactivates the smell days later.

### The Enzyme Cleaner Reality Check
- Enzyme cleaners work **only if they physically reach every place the urine went** — and urine goes deep into carpet padding, sometimes to the subfloor.
- **Spraying the surface is not enough.** The top-voted practical answer: "Pour enough so it fully saturates where the pee went." Not spray — pour.
- **Cover with a towel or plastic wrap and let it sit for hours (overnight if possible).** Enzymes need time, and drying too fast stops them mid-process.
- After: **blot, then let it air dry.** Do not use a fan or blow dryer.

### The Heat Trap (Big Miss in Most Articles)
- **Never use hot water, steam, a carpet-steamer, or a blow dryer on a cat pee stain.** Heat sets the protein and the smell becomes permanent.
- Several comments describe carpet that couldn't be saved because someone used a steamer first.
- Air drying or fans on cool are fine — it's specifically heat that fixes the smell in.

### The "Work Outside In" Technique
- "Start by spraying the area around the stain, then work your way to the center." This prevents wicking — where the stain spreads outward as you treat it.
- Really wet the surrounding area first, then the stain itself.

### The Re-Pee Problem
- Cats detect residual odor at concentrations humans cannot smell. A "clean"-looking spot still smells like a marker to them.
- If your cat re-pees in the same spot, it almost always means the enzyme did not reach all the urine. Re-treat with more volume and longer dwell.
- Masking-fragrance cleaners (florally-scented products) are the worst — they cover the smell for humans but the cat still reads the spot.

### Specific Product Names That Actually Work (Pattern)
- **Nature's Miracle** — classic, reliable, smells somewhat
- **Rocco & Roxie** — strong, divisive ("leaves heavy floral cloud" vs "works wonders")
- **Vital Oxide** — no smell of its own, disinfectant, diluted from concentrate
- **Mister Max Anti Icky Poo (unscented)** — mentioned repeatedly, does not mask
- **MyPetPeed** — hydrogen peroxide based
- **Sunny & Honey Pet Stain & Odor Miracle** — less heavy scent
- **Angry Orange** — frequently mentioned

### Professional Carpet Cleaner Use Case
- Pros have **truck-mount extractors** that pull most of the water/urine out of carpet and pad — home units cannot match the suction.
- Worth calling in if urine has soaked through to the pad or subfloor, or if there are multiple layered accidents.
- **Don't over-saturate the pad with home solutions** — the pad can mildew, and moisture without extraction makes it worse.

### Don't Rule Out a Vet Visit
- Sudden floor peeing (especially by a cat who was previously box-compliant) can be a UTI, kidney issue, diabetes, or stress. A vet rule-out is the first stop, not the last.
- "Behavioral" peeing is real but it is the diagnosis after medical is ruled out.

### Baking Soda as a Co-treatment
- Some use Arm & Hammer enzymatic powder first to absorb moisture, vacuum, then apply foaming enzyme.
- Plain baking soda + vinegar for neutralizing residue of older cleaners (acid + alkaline = easier to extract).

## Angles Competitors Miss

1. **The heat trap.** Most "how to clean cat pee" articles skip over the fact that steamers and blow dryers permanently fix the smell. This is the single biggest mistake.
2. **Volume over spray.** Articles tell readers to "apply enzyme." The actual instruction is pour-to-saturation.
3. **Cover while it works.** Plastic wrap or towel over the treated spot keeps it wet long enough.
4. **Why the cat keeps re-peeing.** Most articles don't explain that re-peeing means your enzyme didn't reach all the urine, not that the cat has a problem.
5. **Urea crystals need acid first.** Vinegar before enzyme is the forgotten one-two punch for set-in stains.
6. **Don't over-saturate the pad.** A lot of DIY cleaners tell people to "soak it." True for carpet, risky for pad — mildew is a real failure mode.
7. **Masking-scent cleaners are worse than nothing.** Because they give the human a false sense of success while the cat continues to read the spot.

## Content Structure (Write Plan)

1. **Quick answer / intro** — Blot heavy, pour (don't spray) enzyme cleaner, cover and wait, air dry.
2. **Why cat pee is harder than other pee** — Uric acid crystals, concentration, vet rule-out.
3. **Step-by-step: fresh stain (within 24 hours)** — blot heavy, pour enzyme outside-in, cover, 4-12 hour dwell, blot, air dry.
4. **Step-by-step: dried or old stain** — vinegar pre-treat to break crystals, then enzyme soak, then repeat if needed.
5. **Finding the smell first (blacklight)** — why a UV light matters, how to use one.
6. **Mistakes that lock smell in permanently** — steam, hot water, blow dryers, masking scents, under-saturating.
7. **When to call a pro carpet cleaner** — soaked pad, multiple accidents, visible wicking rings.
8. **Why your cat keeps re-peeing there** — residual smell detection, not a behavior problem.
