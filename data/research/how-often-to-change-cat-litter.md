## What the Top Comments Actually Say (Pattern Analysis)

### Daily Scooping Consensus
- **1.3K upvotes on "Once a day as bedtime routine"** — the dominant pattern.
- Many scoop **twice daily** (morning + bedtime), especially with multiple cats.
- Consequence of skipping: cat pees in the bathtub, on carpet, on the bed, or near-but-not-in the box. Multiple commenters: "I scooped at 2am because I know they need clean boxes."
- **Analogy that repeats:** "That's like flushing a toilet only once a week."

### Full-Dump ("Deep Clean") Cadence — Reality Check
The #1 top answer (1,285 votes) says **"We deep clean every month. Longer for the unpopular box."** Other top answers echo monthly or every 2 weeks.

This is SIGNIFICANTLY less aggressive than most SEO articles claim (which usually say 1-2 weeks). With good clumping litter + daily scooping, monthly is the real-world norm.

### The Unpopular Box Rule (Major Missed Insight)
In multi-cat homes, one box becomes the "popular" box and the others get used rarely. The popular box needs more frequent deep cleans; the unpopular boxes can go much longer.

### The Sterilization Trap (Critical — Current Page Misses This)
**"This is gross... but I put some used litter from her extra box into the clean one and she starts using it again. It just needs to smell like her."** — 169 upvotes.

**"My cat will immediately run in and do a tiny claiming pee when I've changed the litter."** — 13 upvotes, echoed by many.

**Key insight:** After a total sterile deep clean, cats often refuse the box because it no longer smells like theirs. The fix: keep a small amount of used (but clean-looking) litter from another box as "starter litter." Sourdough analogy is common in comments.

### Heuristic vs Calendar
**"Use your eyes and nose."** — 336 upvotes. The top expert-style answer.
- When pee clumps stop firming up → dump.
- When litter "starts to break down and get dusty" → dump.
- When the smell comes back 30 min after scooping → dump.
- When the cat hesitates or scratches outside the box → dump.

Calendar cadence is a backstop; the real signal is sensory.

### Chemical Safety (Add to Page)
- **Never mix bleach with used litter** — cat urine has ammonia. Ammonia + bleach = chloramine gas. Dangerous.
- Dish soap + warm water is safe and effective.
- Vinegar is debated (safe per some, others recommend plain soap).
- Never use ammonia-based cleaners (cat reads ammonia as urine marker and may re-pee the spot).

### Drying Time
Several comments mention leaving the box in the sun to dry fully before refilling — residual moisture under fresh litter degrades it faster.

### Cost Pushback
**"Every time I read to deep clean every 2-4 weeks I feel we'll be throwing out so much usable litter."** The OP's concern is legitimate. The truth: with good clumping litter and daily scooping, monthly is fine for most homes. Bi-weekly is aggressive and wasteful for single-cat homes.

## Angles Competitors Miss

1. **The sterilization trap.** SEO articles say "deeper is better." Reality: over-sterilizing triggers cat box refusal. Leave some familiar-smelling litter behind.
2. **Unpopular box logic.** Articles give one universal timeline. Reality: in multi-cat homes, boxes have wildly different usage rates and need different schedules.
3. **Eyes-and-nose heuristic.** Most articles push a rigid schedule. The Reddit consensus is: let the litter tell you when.
4. **Chemical safety.** Almost no articles warn about bleach + urine → chloramine.
5. **The claim pee phenomenon.** Cats reclaiming a newly cleaned box with a territory pee is normal, not a problem.
6. **Cost pushback is legitimate.** Many articles push aggressive timelines that waste litter. Monthly with daily scooping is the actual norm among experienced cat owners.
