## Odor Failure Modes Research

Key findings from Reddit/forums on WHY litter fails at odor:

1. **Ammonia breakthrough** - The #1 complaint. Urea in urine is initially odorless, but bacteria convert it to ammonia gas. Multi-cat homes overwhelm litter's absorption capacity faster.
2. **Saturation point collapse** - Crystal/silica litters work great until they hit capacity, then suddenly smell terrible with no gradual warning.
3. **Humidity amplification** - Humid climates make ammonia off-gassing worse. Clumping litters that trap moisture perform better than non-clumping in humid environments.
4. **Scented masking failure** - Heavily fragranced litters just layer perfume over ammonia, creating a worse combined smell. Reddit overwhelmingly prefers unscented with actual odor neutralization.
5. **Clump integrity failure** - Soft/crumbly clumps leave wet litter behind, which becomes a permanent ammonia source at the bottom of the box.

## Key Insights for Writing

1. **Odor control is really 5 different mechanisms.** Most roundups treat "odor control" as one thing. This page should educate readers that baking soda neutralization, activated charcoal adsorption, probiotic breakdown, silica dehydration, and walnut shell absorption are fundamentally different approaches -- and which one works best depends on their specific situation (humidity, cat count, scooping frequency).

2. **The ammonia timeline matters.** Fresh urine is nearly odorless. Ammonia develops over hours as bacteria break down urea. This means scooping frequency interacts heavily with litter choice. A "14-day odor control" litter still needs daily scooping -- the claim means the base litter stays fresh for 14 days, not that individual deposits won't smell.

3. **Multi-cat scaling is non-linear.** Going from 1 to 2 cats doesn't double the odor problem -- it roughly triples it, because cats use each other's boxes and the bacterial load compounds. This is why "multi-cat" formulas aren't just marketing -- they genuinely need stronger odor mechanisms.

4. **The fragrance trap.** Reddit is overwhelmingly anti-fragrance for cat litter. Scented litters mask odor rather than eliminating it, and many cats reject heavily fragranced litter. The page should lean hard into unscented options that use real odor science.

5. **Cost-per-effective-day matters more than cost-per-pound.** PrettyLitter at $3.75/lb lasts 30 days for one cat. Clay at $1/lb needs replacing more often. The real comparison is monthly cost per cat, which narrows the gap significantly.
