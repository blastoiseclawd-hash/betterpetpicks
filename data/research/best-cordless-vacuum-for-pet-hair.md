## Pattern Findings

### 1. Runtime claims vs reality on pet-hair mode

Marketing runtime numbers are always eco mode, usually with no motorized tool attached. The moment you put a motorized pet head on the wand and switch to boost for deep carpet, runtime collapses to 10-20 minutes across every brand tested.

- Dyson V15 Detect: 60 minutes eco, roughly 12 minutes boost mode with the motorized head (Modern Castle, TechRadar).
- Shark Stratos: 80 minutes eco, 20 minutes max mode (TechRadar).
- Shark Vertex Pro IZ682H: 60 min per battery, 120 min total across two batteries — this is the only way to get meaningful "whole-home" runtime on a single charge cycle.
- Tineco Pure One S15 Pet: advertised 40 min, reviewers consistently report shorter real-world life on auto mode (TechHive, Homes & Gardens).
- Dyson V8: around 40 min eco, drops to ~7 min on max with the motor head (Modern Castle).

The buying lesson: a single-battery cordless that advertises 40 minutes will deliver roughly 15-25 minutes of usable cleaning time in a home with pets. If the floor plan is larger than a small apartment or one floor, a dual-battery model is the only honest path.

### 2. Battery degradation after 18-24 months is the real long-term cost

Across aggregated Reddit threads and manufacturer guidance, cordless vacuum lithium batteries lose meaningful capacity on a fairly predictable curve.

- Typical lifespan: 2-4 years, 300-500 charge cycles (Honiture lifespan explainer; ECOVACS guide).
- Owner reports cluster at roughly "30% runtime drop at 6 months of daily use" and "noticeable drop-off by year 2" for heavy-shedder homes.
- Dyson replacement batteries run $100+ and are commonly flagged on Reddit as the reason owners switch brands at year 3 rather than repair.
- Shark batteries on Vertex/Stratos are user-swappable and sold cheaper, which changes the long-term math in Shark's favor for owners who keep vacuums 4+ years.

Editorial synthesis: pet homes accelerate battery wear because boost mode gets used more often, and boost mode is the highest-current drain. A buyer who vacuums daily on carpet is buying a 2-3 year tool, not a 7-year tool, regardless of brand. Frame this honestly.

### 3. Weight fatigue is worse on cordless than on upright

A corded upright rolls on the floor and you push it. A cordless stick gets held in a wand posture — the motor is at the top by your hand, not at your feet. Every ounce compounds over a cleaning session, and on stairs and above-floor work it compounds faster.

- Dyson V15 Detect: 6.8 lb. Lightest of the premium picks.
- Dyson V8: 5.6 lb. Lightest overall among mainstream picks and the reason many users keep a V8 for stairs after upgrading.
- Shark Pet Cordless IX141: 7.9 lb.
- Shark Stratos / Vertex Pro: 8.8-9.7 lb depending on config. The heaviest mainstream cordless options. Users consistently flag "my wrist hurts on stairs" for these.
- Tineco Pure One S15 Pet: ~6.6 lb, comparable to Dyson.

The heuristic that emerges from the threads: under 7 lb is "daily driver for all surfaces including stairs"; 7-8 lb is "fine on floors, tiring overhead"; 9+ lb is "plan your session, don't free-range".

### 4. Dustbin capacity is the stealth constraint in shedder homes

Cordless dustbins are uniformly small — 0.2L to 0.77L. In homes with Golden Retrievers, Huskies, Maine Coons, or any combination of shedders, the bin fills up before the battery dies.

- Dyson V8: 0.54L bin. Smaller bin plus smaller overall frame — flagged in TechRadar and Modern Castle reviews as "emptying every 15 minutes" for shedders. This is the single biggest reason the V8 is not the right V-series pick for pet households.
- Dyson V15 Detect: 0.77L bin. Still small, but noticeably better than V8.
- Shark Stratos/Vertex: ~0.7L class. Similar ballpark.
- Tineco S15: 0.5L / 470ml bin. Reviewers note mid-clean emptying for multi-room sessions (TechHive, Techadvisor).

Pattern implication: the question is not just "how long does the battery last" but "how many times do you stop to empty the bin". Shedder households empty every 10-15 minutes on first pass, every 20-25 minutes on maintenance passes. Buyers do not think to check bin volume in specs, so the buying guide should raise this explicitly.

### 5. Hard-floor vs carpet performance gap widens on cordless

A corded upright has unlimited current and can run a motorized beater bar at full power indefinitely. A cordless stick has to budget wattage between suction motor and brushroll motor. On thick carpet with embedded dog hair, the gap shows up fast.

- RTINGS and Vacuum Wars both rate the Dyson V15 and Gen5detect as "near corded-class on low-pile carpet, noticeably weaker than corded uprights on thick carpet".
- Shark DuoClean PowerFins (Vertex Pro, Stratos) close the gap better than most cordless by splitting the head into soft-roller + bristle, so carpet and hard floor each get a head tuned for the job.
- Budget cordless ($150-$200 range) are best understood as hard-floor-first tools that handle low-pile area rugs acceptably. On plush medium-pile carpet, embedded hair remains after multiple passes.

This is the most important surface-honesty point for the buying guide. Readers assume "cordless that is 'for pets' = handles my carpet" and the honest answer is "depends on your carpet pile and your shedder load".

### 6. Anti-tangle brushroll design is the single biggest differentiator

Every cordless marketed "for pet hair" claims anti-tangle. The actual engineering differs wildly.

- **Dyson V15 / Gen5detect Hair Screw / Motorbar head:** The Hair Screw attachment uses a conical roller that actively channels hair into the bin rather than letting it wrap. The Motorbar head has anti-tangle combs integrated into the main floorhead. This is the best-in-class implementation — multiple reviewers and users describe "never had to cut hair off the roller in 2 years" (Reddit aggregates, TechRadar).
- **Shark DuoClean PowerFins (Vertex Pro, Stratos):** "Self-cleaning" brushroll. Adequate-to-good — Shark's marketing says the bristles don't wrap, and for most users that holds, but long-haired-cat households and some golden-retriever owners report still needing occasional cleanup.
- **Shark Zero-M (older budget Shark):** Earlier generation, less effective. Hair wraps more than current Shark models. IX141 uses this generation of brushroll — a real tradeoff for the $179 price.
- **Tineco anti-tangle comb:** Passive comb that routes hair into bin path. Good on short pet hair, mixed reports for long human hair or long-haired cats (Reviewed, Homes & Gardens).
- **Budget (under $150) brands (Tikom, Honiture, Smoture, Levoit):** "Anti-tangle" is often a marketing label on a near-standard brushroll. Owners still cut hair off every 2 weeks.

The ranking hierarchy is real and buyers can feel it at the 6-month mark. A page selling pet-hair cordless should not flatten this into "all of these are anti-tangle".

### 7. Wall dock vs freestanding is a daily-friction decision

Cordless vacuums that come with a wall dock — Dyson V-series, most Sharks, Tineco — get used more often because they charge vertically in view and invite the 3-minute spot-clean. Cordless vacuums that lean in a closet get forgotten. This is a soft pattern but consistent across ownership threads.

- Wall-dock requires drilling into drywall or a stud, which renters avoid.
- Shark and Tineco ship freestanding charging stands as alternatives in some SKUs.
- Dyson sells a freestanding dock as an accessory at around $130, which buyers discover post-purchase.

Editorial synthesis: the buying guide should nudge buyers to plan storage before purchase. The cordless that lives on a wall near the pet's main zone gets used 3x as often as one stored away.

### 8. Filter washing maintenance is real — and more frequent in pet homes

HEPA and pre-filter washing on cordless is a recurring annoyance in ownership threads, and pets roughly double the required cadence.

- Manufacturer guidance (cross-brand): wash pre-filter every 1-3 months. HEPA every 3-6 months.
- Pet-home cadence (owner reports aggregated from threads): pre-filter every 3-4 weeks is the realistic frequency for a daily-use vacuum in a shedder household. Miss a month and suction drops noticeably.
- HEPA filter replacement (not just washing): every 6-12 months in pet homes. A replacement HEPA costs $15-40 depending on brand.
- Some filters labeled "washable HEPA" still degrade — washing can damage fibers. Replacement, not forever-washing, is often the right call.

Buyers don't know any of this at purchase. Every spec sheet brags about HEPA sealing; none warn about replacement cost or cadence. The FAQ should address this directly.

## Angles Competitors Miss

1. **Runtime math with motorized head, not eco mode.** Most roundups cite the 60-minute marketing number without translating to "13 minutes of actual deep-carpet cleaning". Reframe runtime as "how much carpet can you deep-clean per charge".

2. **Dustbin capacity is the stealth constraint.** No mainstream roundup leads with bin volume. In shedder homes it matters as much as runtime.

3. **Battery replacement is a 2-year line item, not a lifetime commitment.** Acknowledge the $100+ Dyson battery replacement at year 2-3 and the Shark battery swap advantage. Competitors treat cordless as if batteries are eternal.

4. **The V8 is not the right pet vacuum even though it is labeled "Animal".** Despite the name, the 0.54L bin makes it wrong for heavy shedders. Most roundups include it because Dyson markets it as pet-friendly — we should be honest.

5. **Anti-tangle is a spectrum, not a checkbox.** Every manufacturer claims anti-tangle. The real differentiation (Hair Screw > DuoClean > Zero-M > passive comb > marketing-only) is a buying lever most pages don't explain.

6. **Wall storage predicts usage.** Cordless-that-gets-used is the cordless with a dock in view. This is a pre-purchase decision, not a post-purchase accessory.

7. **Filter cadence doubles in a pet home.** Maintenance is rarely priced into "is this vacuum worth it" — it should be.

### FAQs to add (on top of existing four)

- **"Does the Dyson V15 Detect battery last? I've heard they fail after 2 years."** Honest answer: lithium batteries on any cordless degrade. Dyson replacement batteries cost $100+. Shark's batteries are user-swappable and cheaper. If you vacuum daily on carpet, expect 30% runtime drop by month 6 and noticeable loss by year 2 regardless of brand.

For dry pet hair pickup and carpet agitation, a dedicated cordless stick is faster and better at the main job. If you also need wet-mopping, that's a second tool, not a replacement.

### Voice calibration (Pet Cleanup Desk, PERMISSION voice)

- **Lead with permission, not hype.** The first 3 words of the page should give the reader permission to feel what they already feel: "Pet hair is a daily war" or "Your vacuum is not failing you — it was never built for this".
