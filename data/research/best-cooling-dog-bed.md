## Key Pattern Findings

### What Cooling Beds Actually Do
Cooling beds do NOT replace air conditioning or prevent heatstroke. They buffer heat for short periods. Realistic expectations:
- **Gel pads** — 30 to 60 minutes of noticeable cooling before gel equilibrates with body temp. Need 15-20 min unloaded to recharge.
- **Water-filled pads** (K&H Cool Bed III) — hours of sustained cooling because water holds temperature better than gel. Heaviest option.
- **Elevated mesh beds** (Coolaroo) — the only option that provides SUSTAINED cooling via airflow. No recharge needed.
- **Pressure-activated gel** (Green Pet Shop) — 2-3 hours of cooling, recharges when the dog gets off.

### The Tropical / Hot-Home Caveat
In hot, humid environments without air conditioning, none of these beds cool a dog enough to prevent overheating. The bed is a supplement, not a solution. For dogs that live in hot climates, the real intervention is shade + water + AC + limited activity during peak heat. Cooling beds help at the margins.

### Destructive-Dog Problem (Often Missed)
Gel pads and water pads are puncture-vulnerable. Dogs that dig, scratch, or chew at beds will breach them within weeks. The gel is non-toxic but messy. For chewers and diggers, the Coolaroo elevated mesh is the only viable cooling option — nothing to puncture.

### Summer Outdoor Durability
HDPE mesh (Coolaroo) handles sun/rain/dew. The fabric on most gel beds does not — it mildews with extended outdoor exposure. For backyard cooling, go elevated mesh. For indoor AC rooms, any type works.

### Brachycephalic and Double-Coated Breeds
French Bulldogs, Pugs, English Bulldogs, Boxers — brachycephalic breeds overheat faster and benefit most from cooling beds. Huskies, Malamutes, and other thick-coated breeds dump heat through their paws; a cool surface for paw contact matters more than full-body cooling.

### Sizing Mistakes
Buying a "medium" cooling pad for a dog that weighs 60 pounds is common. The pad is smaller than the dog, so the dog lies half-on, half-off. Measure the dog lying on its side — the pad needs to match that length, not the listing category.

### Expected Duration vs Marketing
Listings claim "3 hours of cooling." Real-world reports:
- First 30-45 minutes: noticeable cooling
- 45-90 minutes: mild cooling as gel warms
- After 90 minutes: thermal mass effect only, no active cooling

Most dogs get off gel pads after 30-45 minutes, which is roughly when the cooling effect ends. The pad then "recharges" while unoccupied. This cycle works fine.

## Angles Competitors Miss

1. **Cooling beds are buffers, not solutions.** Most articles frame them as heat-prevention. Set expectations honestly.
2. **Elevated mesh beats gel for sustained cooling.** Articles recommend gel because margins are higher. Coolaroo is often the right answer.
3. **The destructive-dog exception.** For chewers, elevated mesh is the only option.
4. **Sizing matters more than "cooling duration."** A bed too small for the dog is useless.
5. **Outdoor vs indoor beds.** Most articles pretend beds are weather-agnostic. They are not.
