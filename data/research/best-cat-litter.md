## Category Landscape

The broad "best cat litter" market is less about giant price tiers and more about recurring tradeoffs:

1. **Unscented clumping clay remains the safest broad default.** It still wins on scoopability, availability, and day-to-day box maintenance for the average home.
2. **Odor-control claims often come with fragrance backlash.** Many odor-focused products work, but owner frustration around perfume-heavy formulas is real and recurring.
3. **Natural and alternative litters solve some complaints but create others.** Buyers chasing lower dust, lighter bags, or greener materials often run into new issues like tracking, texture rejection, soggier clumps, or a sour/corn smell.

That means the flagship page should not pretend there is one perfect litter. It should recommend the best broad defaults, show why a few alternatives still matter, and make it easy to branch into the more specific spoke pages.

## Final Product Lineup

### Boxiecat Probiotic Clumping Clay Litter
**ASIN:** `B07DK2BQGF`  
**Current street price:** $23.99 / 16 lb  
**Potential badge:** Best Overall

- **Why it's here:** This is the strongest premium-clay candidate for the flagship page because it combines serious clumping performance with broad expert-list support and strong fit for both standard boxes and automation-minded shoppers.
- **Who it's for:** Buyers who want a cleaner-feeling premium clay upgrade and are willing to pay more for stronger clumps and a more refined everyday experience.
- **Who should skip it:** Value-first shoppers who want the lowest cost per pound or households already frustrated by litters that still track more than promised.
- **Reddit sentiment:** Mixed-positive. The tone is usually "excellent clumps, strong box behavior, but still not magic on dust or tracking."
- **Expert coverage:** Cats.com ranked it best overall and gave it a dedicated review, which matters because Boxiecat is easy for broader lists to ignore if they only default to mass-market clay picks.
- **Editorial desk angle:** This is the product the desk can strongly endorse without overselling. It feels like a real step up from grocery-store defaults, but it still lives in the real-home mainstream rather than in a niche luxury lane.

## Key Insights for Writing

1. **Do not write this page like there is one perfect litter.** The job is to help buyers choose the least annoying tradeoff set for their home.
2. **Keep the broad page broad.** Odor-control, multi-cat, no-tracking, dust-free, and automatic-box language should appear, but those problems should still route into their own spokes.
3. **Unscented clumping clay should own the top of the page.** That is still the safest mainstream answer for the first-wave audience.
4. **Fragrance is the biggest trust trap.** A litter can perform well on odor and still be the wrong recommendation for a sensitive household. The guide needs to say that plainly.
5. **Natural alternatives belong in the lineup, but not at the top.** They are meaningful options, not the default answer.
6. **Avoid health-monitoring and diagnostic framing.** That is exactly why PrettyLitter is not a main-page essential here.
7. **Price-per-pound framing matters more than sticker price.** The copy should help readers compare value without pretending a smaller bag with a lower sticker is always cheaper to live with.
