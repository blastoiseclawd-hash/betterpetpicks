## Pattern Findings Specific to Large Cats

### The Weight-on-the-Base Phenomenon
Maine coon owners consistently describe putting extra weight (25-35 lb gym plates) on the base of otherwise-OK cat trees to keep them from tipping. Several anchor their trees to the wall with a cable near the top. This is a clear signal: most Amazon cat trees are undersized for cats 15+ lb, even the "heavy-duty" models.

### What Actually Holds Up
Real-wood construction (Armarkat A7202) comes up repeatedly as the category that does not require aftermarket weighting. Cat Tree King (CTK) and RHR Pets are the premium-tier brands owners point to when money is no object — they stay in good condition for years, but they cost $400-700+.

### Ceiling-Tension Trees
For Maine coon owners, ceiling-tension trees (no drilling, spring-loaded pole pressing between floor and ceiling) are the go-to anti-tipping solution. RHR Pets and several imported brands make them. They remove the wobble problem entirely because the tree is locked vertically.

### Platform Size Is Even More Critical Here
A 15-inch platform fits an average adult cat but not a Maine coon. Large cats need 18 to 22 inches so they can stretch out. Otherwise they hang off the edge or skip the platform entirely and sit on the floor.

### Hammocks That Actually Hold
Cheap hammock fabric tears when an 18 lb cat drops into it. Owners report the fabric edges pulling away from staples. A large-cat tree's hammock should be double-stitched fabric anchored to the structural frame, not stapled to a panel.

## Angles Competitors Miss

1. **The weight-on-base workaround is not a fix.** Competitors recommend "put a weight on it" as if it is normal. Real large-cat trees do not need that.
2. **Ceiling-tension trees exist and solve wobble entirely.** Rare in US affiliate articles but mentioned constantly by Maine coon owners.
3. **Real wood vs MDF is a structural distinction, not an aesthetic one.** Real wood holds a 20+ lb cat; MDF compresses over time.
4. **Hammock fabric failure** is a specific failure mode. Most articles skip it.
5. **The 35-inch base threshold.** For cats over 20 lb, base needs to be bigger than the 19-22" that works for average cats.
