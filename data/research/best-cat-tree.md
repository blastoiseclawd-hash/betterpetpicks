## Pattern Findings

### The Two Failure Modes Owners Describe
1. **Wobble.** Cat tree tips or sways when the cat jumps on/off. Leading cause: top-heavy design with narrow base + tall single post. Happens within first week.
2. **Platform too small.** Cat cannot actually lie down on the top platform. Buyers fail to check platform dimensions before buying and tree becomes visual furniture the cat ignores.

### What Makes a Cat Tree Actually Work (Consensus)
- **Wide, weighted base** beats a tall single post every time. Base width ≥ 20 inches is a good threshold.
- **Platform ≥ 15 inches square** fits most adult cats. Under 12 inches is "too small" territory.
- **Sisal wrap thick enough to claw** (at least 1-inch diameter post with tight sisal rope). Thin cardboard wrapped in sisal fails in months.
- **Replaceable sisal posts** (Chewy Frisco, New Cat Condos) are a quality signal — the brand expects long ownership.
- **Simple path up and down.** Multiple complex overhangs create dead-end platforms cats cannot exit gracefully. Simpler = used more.

### Reputation Patterns by Brand
- **FEANDREA / PAWZ:** Ubiquitous Amazon picks. Quality varies by batch. Best for single-cat homes under 15 lbs. 5-star reviews but consistent cluster of 2-star complaints about wobble after 6 months.
- **Go Pet Club:** Budget multi-level, 62-72 inch trees. Popular for 2+ cat homes. Reviews split on cardboard construction — held up well for years for some owners, broke down quickly for others.
- **Vesper (Catit):** Premium European aesthetic. Real wood components, cleaner look for living rooms. Higher price point. Small single-platform design, not for large cats.
- **New Cat Condos:** Solid-wood construction, more expensive. Owned by multi-cat/large-cat owners who gave up on Amazon. Mentioned repeatedly in sturdy-tree threads.
- **Frisco (Chewy):** Replaceable sisal posts, dense pressboard, heavy plastic support under the twine. A quieter recommendation but consistent.
- **Amazon Basics:** Budget-first, decent starter tree. Works for kittens and small cats. Not built for long-term heavy use.

### The Maine Coon / Heavy Cat Heuristic
"Look for 'Maine coon' cat trees" came up repeatedly. Heavy cats (15+ lbs) need posts thicker than standard, larger platforms, and a lower center of gravity. A standard cat tree will deform under a big cat within months. Large-cat variant buyers report their trees hold up far better.

### Problem Patterns from Owner Reviews
- **Corner of top platform cracks** when the cat jumps heavily. Usually pressboard construction.
- **Sisal loosens and unravels** after 1-2 years. Replaceable posts mitigate this.
- **Carpet fabric pulls loose** from staples. Owners report cats shredding the fabric down to exposed pressboard.
- **Assembly has too many similar bolts.** Common complaint. A design with clearly labeled hardware shortens the 2-hour assembly fight.
- **Box comes damaged** — dent on corner post from UPS handling. Cosmetic but affects resale perception.
