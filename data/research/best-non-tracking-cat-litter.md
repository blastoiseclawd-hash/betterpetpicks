## Key Insights for Writing

### The Physics of Tracking
1. **Granule size is the primary driver.** Large pellets > large granules > fine granules. Anything under ~2mm sticks to paw pads.
2. **Shape matters.** Round/smooth pellets shed from paws. Irregular clay chunks lodge between toes.
3. **Weight is secondary.** Heavier granules fall off paws faster, but size and shape matter more.
4. **Dust amplifies tracking.** Dusty litter coats paws and leaves prints on floors even if the granules themselves don't travel far.
5. **Wet litter tracks worse.** Clay that absorbs moisture becomes sticky. Tofu and pine pellets absorb without becoming tacky.

### Material Hierarchy for Tracking (Best to Worst)
1. **Pellet systems (Breeze-style):** Near-zero tracking. Pellets are too large to leave the box on paws.
2. **Tofu pellets:** Very low tracking. Elongated shape doesn't stick. Stays dry on surface.
3. **Pine pellets:** Very low tracking. Large, heavy. Breaks down to sawdust when wet (sawdust can track).
4. **Large-grain corn/cassava:** Low tracking. Better than clay but smaller than true pellets.
5. **Large-grain clay (Clean Paws style):** Moderate tracking. Better than fine clay, but still clay — gets tacky when damp.
6. **Fine-grain clay:** Worst tracking. Small, sharp, sticky when damp.
7. **Crystal/silica gel:** Varies widely. Large crystals track less, small crystals scatter everywhere.

### The Tracking-vs-Everything Tradeoff
- Pellets solve tracking but many cats reject the texture (especially declawed cats or kittens).
- Pine pellets solve tracking + dust but give up clumping entirely.
- Tofu solves tracking + clumping but costs 2-4x more than clay.
- Large-grain clay reduces tracking without changing the cat's experience much — lowest disruption option.

### Litter Mat as Companion Strategy
