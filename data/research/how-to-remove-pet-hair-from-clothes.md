## Key Patterns from Research

### 1. The dryer pre-cycle is consensus #1
Every thread that discusses laundry mentions running clothes through the dryer first. Key details:
- **Low heat OR no heat both work.** Multiple owners report cold/tumble-only is sufficient — the agitation matters more than heat.
- **10-15 minutes** is the consensus time range.
- **Wool dryer balls improve results.** 4-6 balls recommended. They physically agitate fabric and loosen hair.
- **Dryer sheets reduce static** which helps hair release. Some owners use both balls and sheets.
- **Hygiene concern is real but manageable.** Some people object to putting dirty clothes in a dryer. Response: everyday clothing isn't dirty enough to matter; the dryer heat handles bacteria; if concerned, run an empty high-heat cycle after.
- **Clean the lint trap AND the dryer vent.** Better airflow = more hair captured. A clogged vent reduces suction.

### 2. Washing machine is where hair spreads
- Hair doesn't dissolve in water — it floats, tangles, redistributes
- **Separate pet-heavy loads** from work/dress clothes
- **Liquid detergent beats powder** — powder granules trap hair against fabric
- **Vinegar in the RINSE cycle** (not wash) — softens fibers, releases trapped hair, reduces dryer static
- **Scouring pad hack**: cut up a colorful (non-metallic) scouring pad and throw it in the washer — it catches hair in the wash. Owner-reported, works on long-haired cat shedding.
- **Clean the washer/dryer for "nests"** — if lint rollers aren't helping, a deposit of hair may be building up in the machine itself

### 3. Fabric type determines the right method
- **Fleece/polyester**: worst for pet hair (static), dryer pre-cycle essential
- **Wool**: lint roller pulls fibers — use a garment brush instead
- **Cotton**: releases hair easily, lint roller or damp hand works
- **Silk/delicates**: no dryer, use tape or light lint roller
- **Black clothing**: "I've just stopped buying black" — store separately, "funeral outfit" kept packed away

### 4. Prevention beats removal
- **Deshedding tools > regular brushes** — owner reports massive reduction in shedding on clothes after switching from brushes to deshedding tools
- **Store clothes inside out** — hair hits the inside, flip right before wearing
- **Closed closets and garment bags** — hair can't reach what it can't access
- **Fabric choice** — tightly woven fabrics release hair more easily than fleece/wool

### 5. Quick fixes without tools
- Damp hands in one direction
- Packing tape or masking tape wrapped around hand
- Rubber gloves (same friction principle as furniture method)
- Damp microfiber cloth in circular motion
