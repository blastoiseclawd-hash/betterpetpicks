## Scope

BetterPetPicks should be treated as a broader pet-home utility brand, not a permanently cats-only brand.

That said, this research still supports a cat-first launch because the strongest current evidence, keyword depth, and monetization overlap in the repo sit in cat litter, odor control, water fountains, and cleanup friction.

### Brand-level scope

BetterPetPicks should eventually be able to cover practical pet-home utility products across household animals where the same shopper logic applies:

- mess control
- odor control
- cleaning burden
- maintenance burden
- materials and durability
- convenience and automation

### Cat-first launch scope

The first launch cluster remains:

- Cat litter
- Odor control
- Water fountains
- Litter-area cleanup
- Adjacent cat-home utility products

Still out of scope for wave one:

- Pet food
- Medical or health claims
- Supplements
- Breed-specific care
- Other YMYL-adjacent pet topics

## Important Constraint

The strongest source-backed evidence gathered in this phase is cat-focused. So the right move is not to fake full dog, bird, or small-animal avatar research yet.

Instead:

- define brand-level buyer archetypes that can travel across pet categories
- define the cat-specific launch expressions of those archetypes now
- require fresh species-specific research before expanding into non-cat clusters later

## Research Synthesis

### What the market signal says

1. Pet ownership remains large and younger owners matter more.
   APPA and AVMA summaries point to a durable pet-owning market, with Gen Z and younger owners becoming more important and more likely to shape discovery behavior through social and community channels.

2. The winning angle for BetterPetPicks is household utility, not broad pet lifestyle.
   The strongest signals are about reducing smell, mess, cleanup burden, maintenance friction, and recurring hassle. That logic can extend beyond cats later, but it is already very strong in cat categories now.

3. The current repo evidence is strongest in cat categories.
   Cat litter, automatic litter boxes, odor control, and cat fountains have clear query depth, strong commercial intent, and a direct line to product tradeoffs that fit the BetterPetPicks brand.

4. Real-home annoyance beats novelty across the board.
   Community signals center on the same underlying jobs:
   - set things up correctly without regret
   - manage multiple animals without the house feeling gross
   - reduce dust, tracking, smell, or residue in shared spaces
   - decide whether premium automation is worth the extra money and upkeep

That is the better long-term avatar logic for the brand.

## Brand-Level Avatar Framework

These are the buyer archetypes BetterPetPicks should use at the brand level across future pet-home utility categories.

## 1. Practical Starter

- Core job:
  "Give me the simple default that solves the obvious problem without making me learn a hobby."
- Typical traits:
  Newer owner, lower complexity household, low patience for clutter, and strong preference for straightforward recommendations.
- What they usually care about:
  Easy setup, low regret, low mess, clear value, obvious best-overall picks.
- What they usually do not care about:
  Deep feature analysis, premium design cues, or enthusiast edge cases.

## 2. Household Operator

- Core job:
  "Help me manage more volume, more mess, or more animals without overspending or constantly cleaning."
- Typical traits:
  Multi-pet or higher-load household, more recurring-cost sensitivity, and more concern about how a product performs when routines are imperfect.
- What they usually care about:
  Capacity, odor control, scaling, refill cost, maintenance frequency, durability under heavy use.
- What they usually do not care about:
  Fancy branding or premium features that do not lower total workload.

## 3. Clean-Space Manager

- Core job:
  "Make this product easier to live with in a real shared home."
- Typical traits:
  Apartment dwellers, renters, cleanliness-sensitive households, and buyers annoyed by dust, tracking, slime, smells, noise, or visible clutter.
- What they usually care about:
  Low dust, low tracking, easy wipe-down cleaning, quieter operation, better materials, lower residue.
- What they usually do not care about:
  Feature sprawl that does not improve the daily living experience.

## 4. Convenience Upgrader

- Core job:
  "If I spend more, I want real time savings and less friction, not just prettier gear."
- Typical traits:
  Higher budget, skeptical of hype, interested in automation or premium materials if the payoff is clear.
- What they usually care about:
  Time saved, maintenance burden, app usefulness, failure modes, subscription friction, premium payoff.
- What they usually do not care about:
  Cheapest entry price if it adds chores back into the routine.

### What the brand can expand into later

If later research supports it, the same brand-level avatars can extend into other pet-home utility categories such as:

- pet-hair cleanup
- odor-control tools beyond litter
- pet-safe floor and fabric cleanup
- higher-maintenance hydration or feeding hardware
- automation and cleanup tools for other household pets

Do not expand by assumption alone. Each non-cat expansion still needs its own species/category research pass first.

### What should wait

- Broader pet-hair cleanup expansion as a launch priority
- smart-gadget expansion that is more novelty than utility
- medically framed content around allergies, asthma, urinary issues, or health claims
