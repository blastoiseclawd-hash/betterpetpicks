# BetterPetPicks Desk Image Prompts

These prompts are for transparent editorial desk visuals only. Do not generate human portraits or imply that a desk is a real individual reviewer.

## Global Rules

- Use abstract marks, editorial symbols, or diagram-like icons.
- No faces, people, pets wearing clothes, mascots, or fictional reviewer characters.
- Keep the style clean, flat, and readable at small sizes.
- Use the BetterPetPicks palette:
  - deep ink `#182028`
  - moss `#54785A`
  - clay `#B9773B`
  - light sand `#F5F1E8`
- The visual system should feel practical and household-focused, not cute or childish.
- The launch wave is cat-first, but the symbols should still be reusable for a broader pet-home utility brand later.

## Cat Care Essentials Desk

Prompt:
"Design an abstract editorial desk icon for a practical pet-home utility brand. The symbol should communicate clean routines, low mess, and simple defaults. Use tidy stacked shapes, soft rectangular forms, or an organized tray-like composition. No animals, no faces, no mascots. Flat vector style, restrained palette of deep ink, moss, clay, and light sand. Must feel calm, useful, and easy to scan at favicon-like size."

## Multi-Cat Home Desk

Prompt:
"Design an abstract editorial desk icon for a practical pet-home utility brand. The symbol should communicate scaling, repeated household load, and odor-control systems that still hold up under pressure. Use layered modules, repeating grid elements, or interlocking forms that suggest more than one station working together. No animals, no faces, no mascots. Flat vector style, deep ink, moss, clay, and light sand palette. Must feel sturdy, comparative, and pressure-tested."

## Upgrade and Automation Desk
